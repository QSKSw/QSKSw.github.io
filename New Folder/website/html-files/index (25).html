In December 1995, I came to exist in a terrible desert region of the United States. It was, and continues to be, very hot here.

In 1995 I was diagnosed with a benign Cystic Cerebellar Astrocytoma. To put it simply, brain cancer. It was removed, but left heavy tissue scarring leading to complications requiring the implantation of a medical shunt. There is evidence that I have other conditions, such as selective memory, autism, fibromyalgia, and a multitude of psychological disorders, but insufficient funds have made it difficult for me to seek treatment.
